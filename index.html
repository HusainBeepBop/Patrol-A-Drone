1.0 Executive Summary

This report details the engineering design and systems integration for the Patrol-Pro, a specialized quadcopter UAS built for high-resolution infrastructure and agricultural inspection. The primary objective was to design a platform that achieves a high payload-to-all-up-weight (AUW) ratio while maintaining a professional-grade flight endurance.

The project followed an iterative design process, beginning with a conceptual analysis of a heavy-lift system and then pivoting to a lightweight, highly efficient quadcopter. This pivot was crucial to meet the ambitious target of a 30% payload-to-AUW ratio. The final design represents a significant optimization, resulting in a drone with a corrected AUW of 6.08 kg, a professional-grade payload capacity of 0.83 kg, and a projected flight endurance of over 28 minutes. This report outlines the component selections, engineering calculations, and rationale that led to this optimized final design.

2.0 The Iterative Design Process

The development of the Patrol-Pro was a dynamic, analytical process driven by a specific design constraint: to maximize the payload-to-AUW ratio.

2.1 Initial Concept: The Heavy-Lift Hexacopter (V1)

The first design iteration was a heavy-lift hexacopter based on the Tarot X6 Hexacopter frame. This concept prioritized power and redundancy over all-up weight.

  • Objective: To create a platform with a high power reserve for a wide variety of heavy payloads.
  • Key Components: Airframe: Tarot X4 Quadcopter (1.5 kg), Propulsion: T-Motor U5 KV400, Power System: Tattu 6S 8000mAh LiPo (x2)
  • Analysis & Flaws: The estimated AUW was 14.70 kg, resulting in a low payload-to-AUW ratio of 5.6%. This proved the heavy-lift platform was incompatible with our design goal.

2.3 Benchmarking and Design Pivot

To meet the design constraints, a new, more efficient model was required. The design was completely redesigned, focusing on a minimal-weight quadcopter configuration.

  • Design Rationale: Transition from a heavy hexacopter to a more efficient quadcopter. The goal was to build a system around the payload, ensuring every gram contributed to the efficiency.

5.0 Conclusion

The Patrol-Pro project successfully demonstrates the value of an iterative, data-driven design process. By abandoning an initial, flawed heavy-lift concept, we were able to create a highly optimized and efficient UAS.

The final design, with an AUW of 6.08 kg, a TWR of 4.6:1, and a flight endurance of 28 minutes, provides a robust and reliable platform for high-end inspection tasks. The project's central finding is that while a 30% payload-to-AUW ratio is not feasible with a professional payload of this class, the final design represents a practical and highly capable solution that is ready for systems integration and field deployment. The drone's significant maximum payload capacity of 8.75 kg also ensures it is a future-proof platform.
