1.0 Executive Summary

This report details the engineering design and systems integration for the Patrol-Pro, a specialized quadcopter UAS built for high-resolution infrastructure and agricultural inspection. The primary objective was to design a platform that achieves a high payload-to-all-up-weight (AUW) ratio while maintaining a professional-grade flight endurance.

The project followed an iterative design process, beginning with a conceptual analysis of a heavy-lift system and then pivoting to a lightweight, highly efficient quadcopter. This pivot was crucial to meet the ambitious target of a 30% payload-to-AUW ratio. The final design represents a significant optimization, resulting in a drone with a corrected AUW of 6.065 kg, a professional-grade payload capacity of 0.83 kg, and a projected flight endurance of over 30 minutes. This report outlines the component selections, engineering calculations, and rationale that led to this optimized final design.

2.0 The Iterative Design Process

The development of the Patrol-Pro was a dynamic, analytical process driven by a specific design constraint: to maximize the payload-to-AUW ratio.

2.1 Initial Concept: The Heavy-Lift Hexacopter (V1)

The first design iteration was a heavy-lift hexacopter based on the Tarot X6 Hexacopter frame. This concept prioritized power and redundancy over all-up weight.

  • Objective: To create a platform with a high power reserve for a wide variety of heavy payloads.
  • Key Components: Airframe: Tarot X6 Quadcopter (2.0 kg), Propulsion: T-Motor U5 KV400, Power System: Tattu 6S 8000mAh LiPo (x2)
  • Analysis & Flaws: The estimated AUW was 14.70 kg, resulting in a low payload-to-AUW ratio of 5.6%. This proved the heavy-lift platform was incompatible with our design goal.

2.3 Benchmarking and Design Pivot

To meet the design constraints, a new, more efficient model was required. The design was completely redesigned, focusing on a minimal-weight quadcopter configuration.

  • Design Rationale: Transition from a heavy hexacopter to a more efficient quadcopter. The goal was to build a system around the payload, ensuring every gram contributed to the efficiency.

4.0 Detailed Engineering Calculations

The following calculations provide a more precise validation of the performance metrics for the Patrol-Pro design.

4.1 All-Up Weight (AUW) Calculation

The AUW is the sum of all components.

  • Airframe (Tarot X4): 1.50 kg
  • Motors (4x T-Motor MN4110): 0.608 kg
  • ESCs (4x T-Motor Alpha 60A): 0.248 kg
  • Propellers (4x 16x5.4): 0.112 kg
  • Batteries (2x Tattu 6S 8000mAh): 2.28 kg
  • Payload (DJI Zenmuse H20T): 0.83 kg
  • Avionics (Pixhawk Cube Orange+): 0.072 kg
  • Miscellaneous: 0.406 kg

Total All-Up Weight (AUW): 6.056 kg

4.2 Payload-to-Total-Weight Ratio

This is the key performance indicator for this project.

Formula: Payload-to-AUW Ratio = Payload Weight / Total AUW

Ratio: 13.7%

Conclusion: The final ratio of 13.6% confirms that the initial target of 30% is not practically attainable with a professional payload of this weight. This ratio, however, is a strong, realistic value for a drone of this class.

4.3 Thrust-to-Weight Ratio (TWR)

A TWR above 2:1 is recommended for stable flight and maneuverability. Based on the new data, the maximum thrust per motor is 3.807 kg.

  • Max Thrust per Motor: 3.807 kg
  • Total System Thrust: 4 motors x 3.807 kg/motor = 15.228 kg

TWR: 2.53:1

Conclusion: The TWR of 2.53:1 is still very high, providing a large power reserve for vertical ascent, a high degree of control, and superior stability in high winds.

4.4 Flight Endurance Calculation

This calculation estimates the hover time under ideal conditions, using data from the T-Motor 16x5.4CF data sheet.

  • Hover Thrust per Motor: 6.056 kg / 4 motors = 1.514 kg
  • Total Hover Current: 4 motors x 7.23 A/motor = 28.92 A
  • Total Battery Capacity: 16,000 mAh (16 Ah)

Endurance: ~33.2 minutes

Conclusion: The updated flight endurance is a more precise result based on the manufacturer's data sheet, and it still meets the requirements for a professional inspection mission.

4.5 Maximum Payload Capacity and Flight Endurance

To determine the Patrol-Pro's full potential, we calculate the maximum payload it can lift while maintaining a minimum TWR of 2:1 for safe operation.

  • Max Takeoff Weight (MTOW): Total Thrust / Minimum TWR = 15.2 kg / 2 = 7.6 kg
  • Empty Weight: 5.226 kg

Max Payload Capacity: ~2.374 kg

  • Power at Max Weight: 7.2 kg x 466.808 W/kg = 1867 W
  • Usable Energy (80% rule): 355.2 Wh x 0.8 = 284.16 Wh

Endurance with Max Payload: ~9.13 minutes

Conclusion: The Patrol-Pro has a theoretical maximum payload capacity of approximately 2.374 kg. When flying with this maximum payload, the estimated flight endurance is extended to roughly 9.13 minutes.

5.0 Conclusion

The Patrol-Pro project successfully demonstrates the value of an iterative, data-driven design process. By abandoning an initial, flawed heavy-lift concept, we were able to create a highly optimized and efficient UAS.

The final design, with an AUW of 6.056 kg, a TWR of 2.53:1, and a flight endurance of 33 minutes, provides a robust and reliable platform for high-end inspection tasks. The project's central finding is that while a 30% payload-to-AUW ratio is not feasible with a professional payload of this class, the final design represents a practical and highly capable solution that is ready for systems integration and field deployment. The drone's significant maximum payload capacity of 2.374 kg also ensures it is a future-proof platform.
